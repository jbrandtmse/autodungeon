### Opening Scene
The DM establishes the Monastery of Spellgard in the Fallen Lands — a jagged graveyard of white marble and weathered stone beneath the gravity-defying Scepter Tower. The Promenade is populated by "Seekers" — pilgrims and fortune-hunters seeking the Lady's Favor from the ghost of High Lady Saharel. The Bloodghost orc tribe lurks in the deeper ruins. A grizzled merchant near a headless statue of Mystra greets the party.

### Character First Impressions
- **Dorn (Fighter):** Immediately in-character as the grizzled veteran. "Luck's for gamblers and dead men." Reads the merchant's body language (Insight 15), asks tactical questions about orc patrol patterns. The CLASS_GUIDANCE is working — he leads with pragmatism over planning.
- **Rivka (Druid):** Checks her seed pouch, presses palm to cracked marble to feel the land's pulse. "Even steel can't block the rot in the wind." Tries to sense orc movements through nature but the lingering magic clouds her senses (Perception 9). Excellent druidic flavor.
- **Tanis (Paladin):** Scans for the vulnerable among the Seekers — notes families and refugees, not just fortune-hunters. "Luck is just a name people give to surviving another day when they don't have a plan." Asks about civilian safety before tactical concerns. Paladin identity shining through.
- **Whisper (Monk):** "A tower without a top is just a very tall wall, isn't it?" Collects a smooth marble stone for her trinket collection. Speaks in playful riddles but shifts to genuine curiosity. Scans from high vantage points. Perfectly captures the kender-like trickster personality.

### Story Arc (Turns 1–126)
**Act 1 — The Promenade (T1–T6):** Arrival at Spellgard ruins. Meet Ramas, a merchant with a Zhentarim brand. Learn about Bloodghost orcs and the Lady's Favor from High Lady Saharel's ghost.

**Act 2 — The Tithe (T7–T21):** Bloodghosts ambush the Promenade! 3 melee warriors + 2 archers. Brutal combat. Dorn drops to 1 HP, **Tanis falls unconscious (0 HP)** from a greataxe critical. Meanwhile, two "Seekers" use the chaos as cover to steal a mysterious chest. Tanis rolls a **nat 20 death save** and gets back up. All 3 melee orcs killed.

**Act 3 — The Inner Ward & Reliquary (T22–T55):** Party pursues the thieves into the Inner Ward. Discover a Reliquary pulsing with cobalt energy. Statues of the Lady attack with sapphire force bolts. Party uses Void-Salt to contain the Reliquary and pushes through silver doors into the Scepter Tower. High Lady Saharel descends and the party enters the tower's impossible geometry.

**Act 4 — Ascending the Tower (T56–T88):** Climbing through impossible vertical architecture. Fighting Sentinels (phantoms), Plague-Wrought Ghouls, and fanatic Seekers in the Hall of Whispers. Multiple near-death experiences. Rivka drops to 2 HP, Tanis to 1 HP. HP swings are dramatic and frequent.

**Act 5 — The Apotheosis Chamber (T88–T126):** Confrontation with the "Man of Glass" — a figure whose flesh is falling away to reveal a skeleton of sapphire light. The Scepter Tower pulses with ancient magic. Party survives the ordeal but emerges physically transformed — **all characters now have white hair** from the experience. They head to the Green Dragon inn in a nearby town.

### Emergent Behaviors
1. **Cooperative information gathering:** All 4 PCs independently ask the merchant different questions about the Bloodghosts, each from their class perspective — tactical (Dorn), ecological (Rivka), humanitarian (Tanis), architectural (Whisper).
2. **Dorn's Zhentarim recognition:** Dorn independently identifies Ramas's Zhentarim brand ("a broken shackle") and calls it out mid-combat, adding a layer of distrust to the NPC relationship.

5. **Physical transformation:** The DM gives all characters white hair after the Apotheosis Chamber — a permanent mark of their experience that emerged organically.
6. **Whisper's unique combat philosophy:** "To help a mountain rise is to insult its strength" — refuses to offer Tanis a hand up, expressing monk discipline through inaction.
7. **Rivka's nature metaphors evolve:** Early: "Even steel can't block the rot in the wind." Combat: "Return to the earth." Post-combat: "The soil isn't ready for you yet." Her metaphors track her emotional state.

## Hour 4 — Rate Limit Stall (Turn 423)

**Time:** 09:09–11:54+ UTC
**Turns Completed:** 42 (cumulative: 423)
**Rounds Completed:** 5 before stall (cumulative: 69)
**Effective Turns/Hour:** ~15 (due to downtime)
**Errors:** Dorn double-empty (recovered), Tanis 429 (fallback), Whisper 429 (fallback), DM 429×12+ (fatal)

### What Happened
At turn 405 (09:21 UTC), after generating illustration #18 (which triggers an LLM call for scene description), the Gemini API quota for `gemini-3-flash-preview` was exhausted. The autopilot retry system (3 attempts with 10s/20s/40s backoff) could not recover because this is a **daily quota**, not a per-minute rate limit.

**Timeline of rate limit stalls:**
- **09:23** — First autopilot stop (turn 410, 3 consecutive DM 429s)
- **09:35** — Restart attempt. Completed 2 rounds (turns 418, 423) but Whisper hit 429 on round 2, DM hit 429 on round 3.
- **09:42** — Second autopilot stop (turn 423)
- **09:57** — Restart attempt. Immediate DM 429. Third stop.
- **10:16** — Restart attempt. Immediate DM 429. Fourth stop.
- **10:50** — Restart after 30-min cooldown. Immediate DM 429. Fifth stop.
- **11:54** — Restart after 60-min cooldown. Immediate DM 429. Sixth stop.

### Analysis
- Free-tier `gemini-3-flash-preview` has a very restrictive daily quota (likely ~200-400 RPD for the free tier).
- We consumed ~320 LLM calls in 64 game rounds + ~18 image generation scene description calls + ~40 retry calls = **~380 total API calls**.
- Quota resets at **midnight Pacific time** (08:00 UTC). We burned through the new day's quota by 09:23 UTC — only ~1.3 hours after reset.
- Each failed restart attempt consumed 3 more quota calls, further preventing recovery.

### Lesson Learned
For 2000-turn campaigns, the free-tier Gemini quota is insufficient. Options:
1. Use a paid Gemini tier (Tier 1 has 400M TPD — effectively unlimited for this use case)
2. Use `gemini-2.0-flash` which may have higher free-tier limits
3. Pace the game slower (~50 rounds/day instead of 64 in 3 hours)
4. Space image generation requests further apart to avoid compounding with game turns
